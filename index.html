Dyssynchrony

The synchronicity of cardiac action is important to maintain cardiac output. Diseased hearts often develop dyssynchrony thus worsening the already affected ventricle. Current measurements for dyssynchrony based on Strain and Strain Rate Echocardiography only analyse peak values and tend to ignore the shape and progession of strain throughout the whole cycle.

We developed a new measurement of cardiac dyssynchrony based on inner-vector angles comparing the whole cardiac cycle. Detailed methods and scientific background can be found in Regional Cardiac Dysfunction and Dyssynchrony in a Murine Model of Afterload Stress - Michael Bauer, Susan Cheng, Kazumasa Unno, Soeun Ngoy, Fen-Chiung Lin, Ronglih Liao; Circulation Cardiovascular Imaging; Submitted

We decided to release the Software used to calculate the novel measurement of cardiac dyssynchrony as Free and Open Source Software. It currently supports data exports from Visualsonic and TomTec strain software packages.
